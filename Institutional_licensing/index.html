02 — Licensing Structure

Terms & Conditions

Each licensing tier addresses the specific operational, legal, and capital requirements of institutional deployments under the FORTA Technologies framework.

01 License Scope & Permitted Use

The license grants a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to deploy FORTA technology within the specified infrastructure category and jurisdiction. Permitted use is limited to internal organizational operations unless a distribution rider is executed separately.

  • Internal deployment only unless explicitly expanded
  • Geographic scope bound by jurisdiction agreement
  • Sub-licensing requires written authorization
02 Intellectual Property & Derivatives

All underlying intellectual property remains the exclusive property of FORTA Technologies. Derivative works created during the engagement may be subject to co-ownership clauses as defined in the executed term sheet.

  • Source code and architecture remain proprietary to FORTA
  • Integration adapters owned by licensee upon delivery
  • Derivative IP assessed on a case-by-case basis
03 Compliance & Regulatory Alignment

Licensees operating within regulated jurisdictions must provide evidence of applicable compliance certifications prior to execution. FORTA Technologies maintains the right to audit deployment configurations annually.

  • Data residency requirements honored per jurisdiction
  • GDPR, DPDP, and sector-specific frameworks supported
  • Annual compliance review included in all institutional tiers
04 Revenue Share & Capital Allocation

Capital allocation structures are tiered based on deployment scope and organizational classification. Revenue share models are available for partners with distribution rights or public-sector procurement mandates.

  • Fixed licensing fee structures for defined deployments
  • Revenue share models negotiated for Tier 2+ partners
  • Government procurement frameworks honored where applicable
05 Term, Renewal & Termination

Standard FORTA license terms are issued for 24-month periods with automatic renewal unless written notice is provided 90 days prior to expiry. Early termination clauses are defined within the executed agreement.

  • 24-month initial term, renewable annually thereafter
  • 90-day written notice required for termination
  • Data return and deletion protocol activated on exit

03 — Process Timeline

Engagement Timeline

From initial expression of interest to deployment authorization, the standard FORTA institutional licensing process follows a structured sequence.

Phase 01 · Days 1–2
Expression of Interest Submitted

Partner submits EOI form with organizational details, jurisdiction, infrastructure category, and capital allocation range.

Phase 02 · Days 3–5
Assessment & Qualification Review

FORTA internal review of submitted information against licensing criteria. Qualification status communicated within 48 hours of submission.

Phase 03 · Days 5–7
Term Sheet Issued

Draft term sheet issued to qualified partners outlining licensing scope, capital structure, compliance obligations, and operational boundaries.

Phase 04 · Days 7–10
Legal Review & Execution

Partner legal team reviews and countersigns the term sheet. Executed agreement triggers onboarding and deployment authorization.

Phase 05 · Post-Execution
Deployment Authorization & Onboarding

Access credentials, integration documentation, and compliance audit schedule issued. Dedicated FORTA account contact assigned for all institutional partners.
